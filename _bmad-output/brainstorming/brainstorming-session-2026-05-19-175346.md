### Provocation Technique — Breakthrough Ideas

**Provocation #1: "The resume is dead. Never generate a single resume."**

Useful extraction: Resume builder is the hook, but the real product is AI career coaching + job matching + skill development. Resume = lead gen, coaching = money.

**Provocation #2: "Make it completely free. Forever. Zero revenue from users."**

Useful extraction: Enterprise/B2B monetization — companies pay for access to qualified candidates, universities pay for placement data, employers pay for readiness scores. Users are the product (in a good way).

**Provocation #3: "No website. Only a WhatsApp bot."**

Useful extraction: Mobile-first, conversational UX. Meet users where they already are. Lower barrier to entry. Could be powerful for emerging markets where WhatsApp is dominant.

**Provocation #4: "AI coaches are worse than no coach. Fire all the AI."**

Useful extraction: Pure AI is untrustworthy. Hybrid model: AI handles 80% (formatting, keyword optimization, practice questions), human coaches handle 20% (strategy, negotiation, career decisions). Charge premium for human access.

**Provocation #5: "Charge $1,000/month. Only enterprise."**

Useful extraction: Maybe the individual consumer market is a trap (high churn, price sensitive). B2B-first: sell $12K/year contracts to HR departments for employee career development. Lower volume, higher LTV, lower churn.

**Provocation #6: "Build for career switchers only. Reject everyone else."**

Useful extraction: Career changers are underserved and desperate — they'll pay more for help. Focus on one high-need segment (e.g., "teachers → tech") and dominate it before expanding.

**Provocation #7: "Your AI gets worse the more people use it."**

Useful extraction: Privacy-first approach. Process resumes locally, don't train on user data. Sell "your data never leaves your device" as premium feature for security-conscious users.

**Provocation #8: "The job seeker isn't your customer — their mom is."**

Useful extraction: Parents/partners of job seekers are willing to pay for career help for their loved ones. Gift model: "Give the gift of a career coach" — $49/month gift subscriptions.

- Freemium AI coaching, premium for human access, marketplace for career coaches
- *Ideas: 13, 51*

**Theme 5: Niche-First Strategy** — Dominate one segment first
- Career switchers, specific industries (healthcare, tech), recent grads
- *Ideas: 52, 82, 83*

**Theme 6: Borrowed Pricing Models** — Proven SaaS patterns
- Duolingo ($7/mo), Notion (free→team→enterprise), Canva (free+templates), Calendly (teams)
- *Ideas: 19-25, 57-64*

### Prioritization Results

**Top Priority — B2B/Employer-First:**
- Highest differentiation from existing tools (none do this well)
- Higher LTV, lower churn than B2C
- Aligns with market trend: enterprises investing in career development

**Top Priority — University Channel:**
- Natural entry point for B2B validation
- Alumni outcome data is a unique asset competitors lack
- Recurring contracts with predictable revenue

**Top Priority — Borrowed Pricing Models:**
- Proven patterns reduce execution risk
- Generous free tier → viral adoption → upsell to paid
- Can iterate quickly without custom pricing infrastructure
